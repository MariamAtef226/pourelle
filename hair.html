Food that would Nourish your Hair

Salmon for Shine

Fish like salmon, sardines, and mackerel are packed with healthy omega-3 fatty acids. Your body can't make these healthy fats, so you have to get them from food or supplements. Your body needs them to grow hair and keep it shiny and full.

Grow With Greek Yogurt

It’s packed with protein. Greek yogurt also has an ingredient that helps with blood flow to your scalp and hair growth. It’s called vitamin B5 and may even help against hair thinning and loss.

Spinach to Battle Brittle Hair

It has tons of vitamin A, plus iron, beta carotene, folate, and vitamin C. These work together for a healthy scalp and mane. They keep your hair moisturized.

Guava to Prevent Breakage

This tropical fruit brims with vitamin C. It protects your hair from breaking.

Sweet Potatoes to Fight Dull Locks

Sweet potatoes are filled with a good-for-you antioxidant. Your body turns it into vitamin A. That helps protect against dry, dull hair.

Eggs for Growth

Your protein and iron bases are covered when you eat eggs. They're rich in a B vitamin that helps hair grow. Not having enough of it can lead to hair loss.

Healthy Hair Styles

Low Bun

The low bun is an absolute classic hairstyle for healthy hair. With that bun sitting nicely on the nape of your neck, gravity won’t be tugging on your hair follicles anytime soon.

Pulled-Out Dutch Braid

Braiding can be like marmite: you either love it or hate it. But if you’re not a fan of braids, hear us out. They are the perfect way to create a put-together style whilst protecting your hair from damage.

Half-up, half-down

Half-up, half-down was a big trend a few years ago, but it’s still the perfect hairstyle for healthy hair. The only thing to keep in mind with this style to ensure that it does not become damaging, is to make sure the ponytail is placed towards the back of the head, rather than on top. Otherwise, you will get a similar effect to a high ponytail, which we don’t want!

MESSY HIGH BUN

Sometimes, you need your hair completely out of your face. Whilst we know a high ponytail can cause damage, a high, loose bun is a much better option.

Low Pony Tail

This hairstyle for healthy hair doesn’t need much introduction. If you have never quickly tied your hair into a low pony, where have you been?

Curly Hair Tips (Tried Them Before and Did Well)

1.Don't Overwash

Curly hair is naturally dry and frizz-prone. The natural oils from your scalp that keep straighter hair shiny and moisturized can’t travel down the kinks and bends of a curly hair strand. Shampooing less often can help your curls stay moist, soft, smooth, and healthy. Make sure to use a shampoo designed for curls to help define and detangle your hair.

2.Detangle Gently.

Curls and brushes don’t mix. Detangle your locks gently with a wide-toothed comb and plenty of nourishing leave-in conditioner. Work your way from the ends up to the roots, gently working through snarls and separating curls.

3.Style When Wet

Many styling gels, sprays, mousses, and other products for curly-haired girls are designed to work on wet hair. They’re formulated to penetrate deep into the hair fibers, smoothing, defining, and conditioning for touchably soft curls.

4.Scrunch Those Curls

Cup your locks in the palm of your hand and scrunch your upward, working your curl styling gel, mousse or cream carefully into each strand. Scrunching adds volume and definition to your coils, leaving them bouncy and beautiful.
